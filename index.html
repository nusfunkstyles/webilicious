NUS Funkstyles
A community of funk style dancers
connecting through music and dance.
NUS Funkstyles is aspiring to become a vibrant and prominent tertiary funk style club in Singapore. We have taken part in numerous inter-university and national events, qualifying and even winning a handful of such competitions. Though a relatively young club established in 2011, we are a strong community of funk dancers with a sense of kinship that can be shared through music and dance.

How was the club formed?
Let’s hear from
the founders
A common love for popping and locking, and a shared desire for a conducive environment for dancers to learn and grow while having fun, made NUS Funkstyles what it is today.
- Melissa Lim, Co-Founder, President
We wanted to dance in NUS and realised many like-minded street dancers did too, so it happened rather naturally from random sessions, into a more structured format.
- Louis Kwek, Co-Founder, Dance Captain
The non-toxic dance culture, humility and respect towards every dancer in the dance cyphers - these were what made me decide to embark on this journey with my friends.
- Wang Yingmin, Founding Member, Vice-President
It felt like we naturally transitioned from being a rag-tag group who would consistently gather at (and get chased out of) AS7 to something more structured and catered for everyone.
- Reico Ng, Founding Member, Treasurer
You fall in love. It just happens!
Shih Mu Jung, Founding Member, Secretary
The passion of dance brought together a group of like-minded friends who wanted to spread the funk and freestyle culture (which was not prevalent in NUS then) to NUS!
Lee Zhanhui, Founding Member, Advisor
A bunch of us got together, went a lil nuts, to see how far we could stretch it. It took some effort, but it was worth it.
Yong Tinshen, Founding Member, Advisor
